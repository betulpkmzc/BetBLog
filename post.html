Places to visit in Antalya

Kaleiçi

While walking the narrow streets of Kaleiçi, you can have the opportunity to see the most beautiful examples of Ottoman civil architecture in this geography. Some of the buildings have been converted into boutique hotels. In some, there are cultural venues such as the Suna-İnan Kıraç Museum, cafes, restaurants and souvenir shops. When it comes to entertainment, you can choose two different options, respectively. You can take the excursion boats that depart and have the opportunity to observe Antalya from different angles. In the evening, you can relax in the bars in Kaleici.

Köprülü Kanyon

There are restaurants around the canyon where Köprüçay flows, where you can taste fresh trout. The region also hosts accommodation options for nature lovers.
Apart from rafting, you can focus on ziplining and rock climbing experience in the region. If you wish, you can go for long walks on the safe tracks created on the canyon walls and get great opportunities to explore the environment . Visitors are also offered options such as ATV, unimog and horse safari or cycling tours.

Konyaalti Beaches

After completing your visit to the archeology museum, if you head towards the seaside from Atatürk Culture Park, you can reach Konyaaltı Beach, which many travelers add to their list of places to visit in Antalya to enjoy the sea.
The beach certainly gives its guests the opportunity to cool off during the summer days when Antalya is famous for its heat. For this, you can choose the 4.5 kilometer free section. There are also paid sections on the beach for those looking for alternatives and a variety of services.

Ucoluk

Located in Üçoluk Village of Konyaaltı district of Antalya, the plateau is located at the foot of the Taurus Mountains. Thanks to its cool winds, this place hosts many visitors from the surrounding regions during the summer period, as well as a place where those living in Kemer, Göynük, Beldibi, Konyaaltı and Antalya center prefer to stay from summer to September. It is a good idea to take a tent for accommodation in the village, which is economically and socially active during this period. Camping lovers should add this beautiful plateau to their route.

Karain Cave

Karain Cave, within the borders of Yağca Village, 5-6 kilometers away from the road stretching between Antalya and Burdur, is considered important in terms of both Anatolian and Middle Eastern history. In the cave where excavations have been carried out since 1946, traces of settlement dating back 500 thousand years have been found. As a result of the work carried out in Karain, one of Turkey's largest natural caves, only the objects that are supposed to be used by people in daily life since the Paleolithic Age were not brought to light. In the cave, which consists of seven galleries of different sizes, finds, which are considered to be the first examples of Anatolian art, were also discovered.
